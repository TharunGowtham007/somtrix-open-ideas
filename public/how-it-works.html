How the SomTriX ideas board works

This page explains how the public ideas board is used inside SomTriX. It is not a legal contract, just a clear explanation of the flow.

Public Idea level Community votes

1. What you can do here

  • Share a real problem you see in the world, your city, college or domain.
  • Hint a high-level direction for how it could be solved.
  • Browse ideas from others.
  • Support ideas you would personally love SomTriX to explore.

2. What SomTriX does with these ideas

  • We regularly review the board and look for repeating patterns in problems.
  • Ideas with strong support (votes) are marked as “high-interest directions”.
  • From those, we may pick a small number for explorations like: concept notes, quick prototypes, or limited pilots.
  • When we move forward with any direction, we may communicate it via our official channels (website / socials / direct contact where appropriate).

3. What you should not post here

  • Detailed code, algorithms, or technical tricks that you consider secret.
  • Exact business models, pricing details, or partner lists that are confidential.
  • Anything you might want to patent privately or protect legally in the future.
  • Personal data about someone who has not clearly agreed to be mentioned.

4. Ownership and public nature

  • Everything you post here is text on a public board. Anyone with the link can read it.
  • Do not treat this as an NDA space or a private invention submission portal. It is purely for open, high-level idea discovery.
  • If you have a confidential invention to discuss, that should be done using a different, formal process – not through this public site.

5. How voting works

  • The “Support” button is a simple public vote, not a guarantee that we will build it.
  • More supports = stronger signal of community interest.
  • We combine votes with our own feasibility checks, timing, and strategy before making any decision.

6. If you want to reference this page

You can share this URL with your friends or teammates when you ask them to submit or support ideas. It explains exactly what the board is and is not.

If you are ever unsure whether something is safe to post here, it’s safer to keep it private and only share a simpler, high-level version of the idea.
